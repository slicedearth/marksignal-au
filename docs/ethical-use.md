# Ethical use

MarkSignal AU is designed for evidence-first corporate research using public records.

## Required interpretation

A trade mark filing is not confirmation that a product or service will launch. It is also
not evidence of infringement, wrongdoing, financial performance, ownership of a company, or
an endorsement by IP Australia.

Users should inspect the linked primary record, consider the complete event history, and
seek appropriate professional advice before making legal, commercial, or investment
decisions.

## Publication limits

- Focus on organisations rather than private individuals.
- Do not republish addresses, personal email addresses, phone numbers, or agent details.
- Separate published facts from calculated observations.
- Describe name and logo relationships as similarity, not copying or infringement.
- Treat new classes and filing clusters as leads that require context.
- Provide enough evidence for another researcher to reproduce or challenge a result.
- Correct false entity matches promptly and preserve a regression test.

## Appropriate uses

The project can support journalism, competitive research, brand monitoring, public-interest
research, and source-system quality checks. Harassment, doxxing, deceptive impersonation,
automated legal accusations, and attempts to identify private individuals are outside its
intended use.
